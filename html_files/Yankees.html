History

The New York Yankees, founded in 1903, are the most successful franchise in Major League Baseball, playing at Yankee Stadium in the Bronx. Nicknamed the “Bronx Bombers,” they have won a record 27 World Series championships, led by legends like Babe Ruth, Lou Gehrig, Joe DiMaggio, and Derek Jeter. The Yankees’ dominance through multiple eras, from the Ruth–Gehrig dynasty to the Core Four of the late 1990s, has made them a global sports icon. Today, behind stars like Aaron Judge and Gerrit Cole, the Yankees remain perennial contenders and a symbol of baseball excellence.
